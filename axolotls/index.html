All About Axolotls


What is an axolotl?

Axolotls are a type of amphibian in the salamander family.

Where are they found?

Axolotls are found in Lake Xochimilco, found in the Valley of Mexico by Mexico City. They are a critically endangered species.

What about in capitivity?

Axolotls thrive in the pet trade and in lab settings due to their high desirability. They're studied extensively due to their ability to regenerate limbs.

Where could I get one?

They can be obtained from reputable breeders and kept safely by enthusiasts, but beware! Tank setup and care is not a simple thing. Do not buy an axolotl if you do not intend to care for them.

How long do they live?

Axolotls can live up to 15 years! They are a commitment and will require a lot of attention.

Where do they live?

Ideally, a single axolotl should have 20 gallons of water. They are cold water amphibians and therefore you will need either a cold room to keep them in or a chiller for the tank.


Adult axolotls can cohabitate! You should aim for a base of 20 gallons + however many tankmates your axolotl has. So if you have two axolotls, you should have a 30 gallon tank, and if you have three, you should have a 40 gallon tank.
